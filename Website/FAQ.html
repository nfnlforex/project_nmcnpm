13. How to find if a journal is Scopus indexed?

Scopus-indexed journals are highly reputed and attract not only genuine researchers but many fraudsters also. Many scopus journals lie about being registered as Scopus-indexed. So, it is crucial to verify their authenticity. If you want to know whether or not a journal is Scopus-indexed, simply visit the official Scopus website (www.scopus.com) and navigate to the Journals tab. On the Journals page, you can find specific journals by title, ISSN, or publisher.

Once you find the journals in your industry, check the list carefully and verify their inclusion in the Scopus database. The "Indexed in Scopus" badge is a confirmation that the journal is authentic, as it is on Scopus' official website. Another verification method is to check the regularly updated Scopus Source Title list. Additionally, book Title List is a method that allows you to download indexed book titles in Scopus.

15. How do I know if my journal is SCI or Scopus?

SCI (Science Citation Index) is a part of the Web of Science and offers all journals related to this one niche. You can find a list of these journals on the official SCI website. The major difference between SCI and Scopus is that the latter is a platform that offers journals in various niches and over a thousand journals.

Scopus usually docs on more independent journals in technology, medicine, and science, and SCI focuses on technical and rigid scientific publications. These journals cover various topics and satisfy a particular reading segment. You can check www.scopus.com?source and find a list of Scopus-indexed journals. If your journal is mentioned here, then it is a Scopus journal. Alternatively, you should use the journal's ISSN number and check if it is listed in Scopus or SCI.
